My Videos

Motion Capture Test (2026)

Created as a project for a class, this video showcases two futuristic men have a pretend shoot off, which accidentally ends in tragedy. Animated using Motive, MotionBuilder, and Unreal Engine. Audio and Video done in Adobe Premiere and REAPER.

Grief: Love With No Place To Go (2025)

Directed and produced for Sessions Cafe TV, Grief is an award-winning short documentary exploring Keri Singleton’s emotional journey following the loss of his father. The film focuses on the personal process of navigating grief and reflection through an intimate interview with an end-of-life doula. Edited using Adobe Premiere Pro.

Life (2025)

Created using nearly 1,000 unique still images, Life tells a short story following a man’s journey from birth to death. Photography was captured using Dragonframe, with post-production completed in Adobe Premiere Pro.

Mando Hates Dubstep (2020)

Inspired by the Season 2 finale of The Mandalorian, created a fan-edit blending key moments from the episode with a royalty-free dubstep track as a stylistic callback to the viral Star Wars edit “The Techno Union.” The project focuses on rhythm, pacing, and synchronization between visuals and music, and it has amassed over 170,000 views on YouTube. Edited using Adobe Premiere Pro.

Future Casting Project (2020)

Created an experimental video for a class project exploring the concept of a hypothetical full-body haptic feedback VR suit. The suit was first modeled in Blender and then imported into Unity for integration. Final footage was captured in VRChat, using the virtual environment as a testing and performance space, before being edited in Adobe Premiere Pro.

The Two Most Controversial Rules in Rehydrated Speedrunning History (2020)

Produced a documentary-style video outlining two rules within the Spongebob: Battle for Bikini Bottom Rehydrated speedrunning community. Using footage sourced with permission from original creators, I aimed to replicate the informative and analytical style commonly seen in speedrunning-focused content. The project emphasizes clarity, pacing, and community context. Edited using Adobe Premiere Pro.

VR Twins (2020)

Created a narrative video exploring the concept of a person’s actions being controlled by an external force, inspired by Being John Malkovich. Filmed remotely during the COVID-19 quarantine, the project required careful coordination to plan shots, align performances, and visualize the final edit without in-person collaboration. Edited using Adobe Premiere Pro.

Just WHAT Troubles Them Today? (2020)

Created an experimental video produced entirely within Overwatch, using the game environment as a virtual film set. The project explores concepts from an AP Macroeconomics unit, with all footage captured in spectator mode. I directed an in-game performer to pantomime as the on-screen host while providing voiceover narration, blending gameplay visuals with educational content. Recorded in Overwatch and edited using Adobe Premiere Pro.

Confuseral (2019)

As director, cinematographer, and editor, I produced a satirical infomercial inspired by 1984, centered around a fictional product. The piece blends classic “as-seen-on-TV” infomercial aesthetics with scripted skits, marking several first-time creative and technical experiences. Edited using Adobe Premiere Pro and Adobe After Effects.
